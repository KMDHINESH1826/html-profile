Noodles

JNoodles are a type of food made from unleavened dough,
typically wheat or rice flour,
that is rolled flat and cut into various shapes, or extruded.
They are cooked by boiling and can be served in soups,
stir-fried, or used in a variety of dishes across many cuisines worldwide.

Pasta

Pasta is an Italian staple food made from durum wheat flour mixed with water or eggs,
formed into various shapes, and cooked by boiling or baking.
Common varieties include spaghetti, penne, and macaroni,
often served with sauces or in baked dishes.

Raman

Ramen is a Japanese noodle soup dish consisting of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a meat or fish-based broth,
often flavored with soy sauce or miso,
and topped with ingredients such as sliced pork,
nori (seaweed), menma (bamboo shoots), and green onions.
